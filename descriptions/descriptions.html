Introducing Kramden: The Nonprofit That Is Bridging The Digital Divide

Kramdens purpose is to connect North Carolinians in building digital equity. That means providing refurbished devices that the nonprofit receives from individuals and business donors or using business sponsorships to provide devices and technology education to those who have not had it. Kramdens community include:

  • - K-12 students who do not have a computer at home
  • - Low income adults who have not had computer education at school
  • - Nonprofit organizations that provide STEAM education (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts & Mathematics)

One of Kramdens major initiatives is "digital literacy programming" in which the nonprofit brings individuals to Kramden or geeks (Kramden volunteers) go out into the community and talk with and assist individuals on how to use and understand their computers. At the end of these workshops participants receive a computer and get to take it home!

    Introducing The Resiliency Collaborative: Making A Profound Difference In The Lives Of BIPOC Youth

    North Carolina is ranked 42nd among states with the highest prevalence and lowest access to mental health services for youth, and suicide is now the second leading cause of death for North Carolina youth ages 10 - 18 (NCDHHS, 2024).

    BIPOC youth with mental health challenges are more likely to be directed to the juvenile justice system than non-Latinx white youth. They face higher rates of harsh disciplinary and expulsion practices that divert them to the juvenile justice system, as opposed to appropriate mental health services (American Psychiatric Association).

    The Resiliency Collaborative (TRC) was established in the Spring of 2022 in response to youth requests for mental health, healing justice and a youth-led space. The organization works alongside BIPOC youth in Wake County, North Carolina to foster a sense of belonging, promote youth voices and foster the capacity to thrive and drive equitable change.

    TRC offers the following programs for BIPOC youth: Leadership Development, Educational Equity & Academics and Mental Health & Wellness.
