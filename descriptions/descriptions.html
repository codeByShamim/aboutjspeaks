Wisdom Vs. Knowledge

I had the privilege to speak to both middle schoolers and adults at "Durham Divinity School" on the difference between Wisdom and Knowledge. It is important for all of us to utilize our Godly wisdom in all things and always make a conscious effort to do what is right in order to fulfill the will of God.

Channel Your Godly Wisdom

Be open to building an intimate relationship with God in order to get the best results in life. As the word of God says, trust in the LORD with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways acknowledge him, and he will make your paths straight” (Proverbs 3:5,6). Always choose to be discerning and channel your Godly wisdom in times of distress. Don’t let your emotions control you and have you react to everything without taking time for proper prayer, silence and meditation. When you grow in your ability to discern and can effectively channel your Godly wisdom you will avoid so many potential potential problems.

Be The Victor Not The Vicim

Strive everyday to live an intentional and victorious life. No matter how small, set out measurable goals that you hold yourself accountable in and do all that is necessary to give yourself a favorable review everyday.

Don't let your emotions dictate your happiness and gratitude towards all of the wonderful things that God has done (and will continue to do) in your life. Our emotions urge us toward haste, telling us that we must do something and or feel a certain way right now. Something that is so unstable and fleeting shouldn't solely determine how grateful you are of your now and all of the beautifulness that is in front of you.

Live In Your Present And Enjoy Your Now

Often times we let our past and hopes for our futures distract us from living in our present and enjoying our now. No matter what we are going through in life it shouldn’t be too great that it distracts us from appreciating all of Gods wonderfulness. Yes, keep your eyes on what’s ahead of you and what you wish to accomplish but take ample time to consider, ponder about and appreciate your now and how far God has brought you in your life.

Negativity Can Not Foster Holiness

Your environment and who you surround yourself with should be sacred to you. Don’t waste your time hanging around people who make seemingly impossible situations even worse. Instead seek fellowship with God. Stay grounded in Gods word and his promises over your life!

Let Your Mind, Body And Soul Win

Always prioritize being full and content in your physical, mental and divine health over chasing money and external accomplishments when it comes to being happy. Don’t get caught up in how the world defines your happiness - through materialistic gains and money. Instead strive to surround yourself with individuals who uplift you, work towards functioning well in the world and point more people to Jesus Christ!

Make Gods Timing Your Timing

It is so easy for us to get frustrated with our best. We have to remind ourselves that when we embark on any journey there is always a starting point. Something that we aspire to grow in, be more proficient in and master - will take time, there is no way around it. To make this inevitable journey, this building period, more bearable on our psyche and spirit we must be patient and trust in the lord with all our heart and lean not to our own understanding but in all ways submit to him and he will straighten our path (Proverbs 3:5 - 6).

Pride Leads to Destruction

There are so many takeaways from this avoidable tragedy that we can all learn from. Being open to hearing and instilling others advice and constructive criticism is a favorable and mature habit. The word of God says: do nothing from selfish ambition or conceit, but in humility count others more significant than yourselves (Philippians 2:3).

No matter how right or assured we are within ourselves, I urge you to be open to the advice of others and seek God with an open heart so that God can reveal himself to you - show you his grace, mercy and answer your prayers. God does not reveal himself to prideful people because arrogant people don’t want to know him. The word of God says: God oppresses the proud and gives grace to the humble (James 4:6).

Always Do The Right Thing

Think of a time you acted on impulse. How did that work out for you? Do you think that was right thing to do? Whenever you have the urge to fight fire with fire please turn your focus to God - he will never forsake you.

    Introducing The Resiliency Collaborative

    North Carolina is ranked 42nd among states with the highest prevalence and lowest access to mental health services for youth, and suicide is now the second leading cause of death for North Carolina youth ages 10 - 18 (NCDHHS, 2024).

    BIPOC youth with mental health challenges are more likely to be directed to the juvenile justice system than non-Latinx white youth. They face higher rates of harsh disciplinary and expulsion practices that divert them to the juvenile justice system, as opposed to appropriate mental health services (American Psychiatric Association).

    The Resiliency Collaborative (TRC) was established in the Spring of 2022 in response to youth requests for mental health, healing justice and a youth-led space. The organization works alongside BIPOC youth in Wake County, North Carolina to foster a sense of belonging, promote youth voices and foster the capacity to thrive and drive equitable change.

    TRC offers the following programs for BIPOC youth: Leadership Development, Educational Equity & Academics and Mental Health & Wellness.
