The Facts And Fiction About Your Mental Health


Dr. Paige Bentley is an “aliveness seeker” on a mission to restore joy, peace, and well-being in the world, one individual at a time. As the Director of Counseling & Well-Being Services and Assistant Professor in the Department of Psychiatry at Wake Forest University School of Medicine, she also runs a private practice offering counseling and coaching for individuals seeking to align their lives with their values and purpose. Dr. Bentley holds a Ph.D. in Counseling & Counselor Education and is a Licensed Clinical Mental Health Counselor Supervisor (LCMHCS), an EMDR certified therapist and consultant-in-training, a Somatic Experiencing Practitioner, and a Registered Yoga Teacher.


Known for her energetic, playful, and heartfelt presence, Paige is trained as a holistic stress management instructor by the National Wellness Institute. She believes that true learning and change occur when we engage our whole selves—mind, body, and spirit. Her well-being practice incorporates a variety of approaches, including mindfulness, play, and improvisation, all aimed at creating brave spaces where clients can explore their identities, aspirations, and the steps needed to reach their goals. Additionally, Paige is a published author and sought-after speaker on topics related to well-being, such as applied mindfulness, flourishing, compassion, and burnout.


Before joining Wake Forest Medical School, Dr. Bentley served as the Associate Director/Clinical Director of Health and Counseling Services at the University of North Carolina Asheville and as a Senior Mental Health Counselor at the University of North Carolina School of the Arts. With a decade of experience in journalism and public relations, she later returned to Wake Forest for her master’s degree in counseling and obtained her Ph.D. from the University of North Carolina Greensboro.
